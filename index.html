About The BMW R75

The BMW R75 is a World War II-era motorcycle and sidecar combination produced by the German company BMW.

The BMW R75 stands out by its integral two-wheel drive design, with drive shafts to both its rear wheel and the third side-car wheel, from a locking differential, as well as a transfer case offering both road and off-road gear ratios, through which all forward and reverse gears worked.

This made the R75 highly manoeuvrable and capable of negotiating most surfaces. A few other motorcycle manufactures, like FN and Norton, offered optional drive to sidecars.

The BMW R75 historically was used by the Werhmach not just for rear echelon duties such as supplies and communications but as recon and frontline duty by chasing retreating enemy forces.

Motorcycle Troops engage while mounted with the attached GPMG MG34/42 on the motorcycle, or dismounted with their personal weapons and the motorcycles MG34/42.
